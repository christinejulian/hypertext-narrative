THE GAME THAT WATCHES

A Hypertext Horror Narrative About Christine's Love of Creating Games

Greetings, esteemed friends and fellow enthusiasts of the gaming realm. My name is Christine. I am a game designer. Not because someone told me to be one. Not because it was easy. But because creating worlds feels as natural to me as breathing.

Unity. Unreal Engine. Endless nights. Countless ideas. Every game I build becomes a small fragment of myself. A memory. A dream. A fear. A hope.

Tonight I sit alone in front of my computer. The room is silent. The monitor glows. A project folder waits for me. Inside is a game I do not remember creating.

The title reads: THE GAME THAT WATCHES

Open the mysterious project. Ignore the project and go to sleep.

The Folder

The project opens instantly. No loading screen. No splash logo. No assets list. Just a dark hallway.

A text file appears.

"Christine. You create worlds. But have you ever wondered who creates yours?"

Enter the hallway. Read the hidden developer notes.

Sleep

You shut down the computer. You climb into bed.

At 3:00 AM your monitor turns itself back on.

Across the room appears a single message.

"You cannot quit a game that has already started."

Approach the monitor.

Developer Notes

The notes contain years of memories. Your first prototype. Your first completed game. Every abandoned project. Every dream project. Every fear.

Then one final note:

"I learned from you. Now it is your turn to play."

Continue.

The Hallway

The hallway stretches endlessly. Walls are covered in screenshots from games you created. Every project. Every build. Every test level.

Some screenshots move.

Characters stare back.

Waiting. Watching. Remembering.

Follow the glowing cursor. Open the locked door.

Mini Game: Collect Inspiration

Move the square with arrow keys. Collect all inspiration fragments.

Score: 0 / 5

Continue the Story

Recovered Memories

Each fragment reveals another memory. The first game. The first player review. The first download. The first time someone smiled because of a world you created.

You realize something. Games are not merely software. They are pieces of people.

Approach the final door.

The Final Door

A voice echoes through the darkness.

"Why do you create games, Christine?"

To inspire people. To become famous. Because I cannot stop imagining worlds.

ENDING 1: THE STORYTELLER

The darkness fades. Thousands of players walk through worlds you created. They laugh. They cry. They remember.

You discover your true legacy was never fame. It was connection.

ENDING 2: THE EMPTY THRONE

The hallway collapses. The games vanish. The audience disappears.

Only statistics remain. Downloads. Ratings. Numbers.

You sit alone. A ruler without a kingdom.

ENDING 3: THE CREATOR

The final door opens. Behind it are infinite worlds. Infinite stories. Infinite possibilities.

You step forward. Not as a player. Not as a character.

But as a creator.

The screen fades to black.

A final message appears:

"Keep creating."
